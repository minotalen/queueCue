    How does it work?

    What is QueCue?

    QueCue is a speaker queue tool for groups that want to keep their (online) meetings fair and organized.
    Users raise their hands to get added to the queue of upcoming speakers. The current speaker (or moderator) then hands the speaker role over to the next person in the queue so they can speak.
    If users have a question or remark for the current speaker they can raise a direct reply to 'skip ahead' in the queue and reply directly to the speaker.

    Give a Direct Reply

    A direct reply  skips the regular queue, enabling you to reply to the current speaker.
    This feature is here to help finish a topic before starting a new one.
    You can raise and lower your direct reply hand by clicking on . Keep it short and sweet! When you are finished the current speaker can cue  to the next direct reply or speaker.

    Moderating

    The first participant to join a room becomes moderator.
    They can manually cue the next speaker (). This is mainly a fallback option, ideally the participants of your meeting cue the next speaker themselves.
    They can also view and configure room settings and check the event log.

      Queue Softness:

    A quote softness of 0 means that when a person who has spoken fewer times raises their hand, they will move forward in the queue. This way people who have not yet had the chance to contribute get moved ahead. Increasing the softness increases the threshold for skipping ahead, so a moderator can increase it to skew the queue towards chronological order.

      Remove On Leave:

    Enabling 'remove on leave' removes users that disconnect from the queue and user list. Disabled by default, so users that lose connection can simply reconnect by entering their username. A user that reconnects while 'remove on leave' is disabled keeps their queue positions and speaker/direct role. In case a user disconnects and fails to reconnect, the moderator has to skip them when they would become speaker.
